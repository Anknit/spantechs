CSR:A Tool For Sustainable Development

In the age of globalization, corporations and business organizations are no longer confined to the traditional boundaries.Acompany'ssuccess is the result of combined efforts and the level of satisfaction of the enterprise’s Stakeholders, including, not only the owner(s) but also employees, customers, suppliers or the local community. Companies begin to understand that they need to operate in a complex network society to become successful,and also that social and environmental  issues are part of that complexity and constitute factor of sustainable Success. Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) is an approach whereby companies integrate social and Environmental concerns in their business operation and in their interaction with their stakeholders on a Voluntary basis. Now CSR is understood as a strategic means and management tool to enhance the Competitiveness of the corporates.

CSR is not a new concept in India. Ever since their inception,mojor corporates have been involved in serving the community through donations, charitable events and other philanthropic activity. Many organizations have been doing their responsibility for the society. The basic objective of CSR is to maximize the company'impact on the society and stakeholders. Business house feel that CSR is not just another form of indirect expense but is Important for protecting the goodwill and reputation and increasing business competitiveness and market Penetration.

The CSR provisions as per the Indian Companies Act,2013 are applicable to companies with annual turnover of INR 1,000crore and more or a net worth of INR 500 crore and or a net profit of INR 5 crore and more.The company has to spend, in every financial year, at least 2% of average net profit of the company made during three immediately preceding financial years.
